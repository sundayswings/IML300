public space!
a lived space!

you see them and they see you

as if that boundary dissolves, recedes, temporarily put on hold

an encounter suddenly becomes so easy

it's so natural to linger on, aimlessly
feeling involved by simply gazing

a lost aesthetic of everyday life,
                                made invisible by the walls of privacy and weariness of city life


                or is it too quick to jump to describe it as a 'lost aesthetic'?
