陳炯明 Chʻên2 Chiung3-ming2

Born in Haifeng, Guangdong in the late Qing Dynasty, Chen Jiongming was best known as a Haklau revolutionary figure who, during his political career, played a key role in the tumultuous politics of southern China from 1909 until his death in 1933. Defeated by Sun Yat-sen’s forces, Chen fled to Huizhou in the eastern Guangdong and then Hong Kong, and died of typhus in this foreign land. He was buried in Huizhou a year later.

In 2012, 79 years after his passing, a band from Haifeng wrote a song about him. The entire song repeats the four lines.

1878年，伊生于海丰（海丰话）
In 1878, he was born in Haifeng (Haifeng dialect, a regional variation of Hokkien).
1933年，佢死于香港（廣東话）
In 1933, he died in Hong Kong (Cantonese).
1934年，其葬于惠州（客家话）
In 1934, he was buried in Huizhou (Hakka).
